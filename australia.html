Why Study in Australia

Australia offers world-class education in a relaxed, sunny environment — with universities like Melbourne, Sydney, and ANU consistently ranking in the global top 100. The "work while you study" policy allows 48 hours of work per fortnight during semesters, helping offset living costs. Australia's strong economy in mining, healthcare, and technology creates abundant opportunities, while the Temporary Graduate Visa provides 2–4 years of post-study work rights with well-established skilled migration pathways.

Popular Courses

Marine Biology & Environmental Science

Australia's unique ecosystems — including the Great Barrier Reef and vast outback — make it a world-leading destination for environmental and marine science. Universities like James Cook and Queensland offer unrivalled field research access and produce graduates driving global conservation efforts.

Hospitality & Tourism Management

Australia's thriving tourism industry — one of its largest economic drivers — creates exceptional demand for qualified hospitality professionals. Programmes at institutions like William Angliss and Griffith University combine theoretical knowledge with industry placements in world-class resorts and hotel groups.

Healthcare & Medicine

Australian medical and healthcare programmes are globally respected for their high standards, modern facilities, and clinical training. Universities like Melbourne, Sydney, and Monash offer strong pathways into Australia's healthcare system, with chronic demand for nurses, doctors, and allied health professionals.

Business & Accounting

Australia's position as an Asia-Pacific trade hub makes it ideal for business and accounting studies. Top institutions like UNSW, Melbourne Business School, and Macquarie offer programmes with a strong focus on regional commerce, finance, and CPA-aligned accounting qualifications recognised across the Asia-Pacific.

Engineering & Mining Technology

Australia's vast natural resources and booming mining sector create unmatched demand for engineering graduates. Universities like Western Australia and Curtin lead in mining, civil, and mechanical engineering, with direct industry partnerships and some of the highest graduate salaries in the country.

Visa Overview

The Australian Student Visa (Subclass 500) allows you to study full-time at a registered CRICOS institution. Known for its transparency and efficiency, the application process is straightforward — and post-study work rights are among the most generous in the world.

01

Get CoE Letter

Receive Confirmation of Enrolment from your CRICOS institution

02

Prepare Documents

Financial proof, English scores, health insurance (OSHC) & health exam

03

Apply Online

Submit Subclass 500 application via ImmiAccount portal

04

Fly to Australia

Begin your academic journey Down Under

💰 Funds: AUD $21,041+ per year demonstrated
🗣 English: IELTS / PTE / TOEFL required
🏥 Insurance: OSHC mandatory for full visa duration

After Graduation

Temporary Graduate 2–4 years post-study work (Subclass 485)
Skilled Independent Points-based permanent residency (Subclass 189)
Employer Sponsored Employer-nominated visa pathway (Subclass 186)
Australian PR Clear pathway to permanent residency & citizenship
